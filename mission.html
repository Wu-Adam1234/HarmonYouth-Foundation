Our mission

We started this because most senior homes don't get much live music, and the people there still love it.

HarmonYouth Foundation wants live music to be a normal part of life at senior homes, not something that only shows up once a year around the holidays.

Most senior homes only bring in entertainment once in a while. A holiday concert, maybe a choir that visits once a year. We wanted something that shows up on the calendar every couple weeks instead, so residents actually start looking forward to it.

We also wanted to give student musicians somewhere to play after their last recital or exam is done. Nobody has to commit to every single date. You sign up for whatever works for you, and you get a real audience instead of a panel scoring you out of ten.

All we ask from a home is a room, some chairs, and a date we can count on.

How a show comes together

The same four steps, every couple weeks.

01

Someone reaches out

Usually it's an activities coordinator getting in touch, or us following up with a home we already visit, to lock down a date.

02

We check who's free

Everyone on our list gets asked if they can make that date. Whoever says yes, plus a few more, that's the lineup.

03

We show up and play

Usually 30 to 45 minutes, a mix of solo pieces and a few group ones, in whatever common room the home gives us.

04

We follow up

If people are okay with it, we post a few photos afterward. It's usually what convinces the next home, or the next musician, to give us a shot.
