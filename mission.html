Our mission

Music doesn't stop mattering once you're not the one performing it anymore.

HarmonYouth Foundation exists to make live music a normal, recurring part of life in senior care homes, not an occasional special event.

Most senior care homes host entertainment occasionally: a holiday concert, a visiting choir, an annual event. We wanted to build something that shows up on the calendar regularly instead, so residents can count on it the same way they count on any other weekly activity.

We also wanted to give student musicians a reason to keep playing past their last recital or exam. A rotating roster means performers don't need to commit to every date, but they get a real audience whenever they're available.

We are not a fundraising organization and we do not charge homes or residents for performances. The only thing we ask of a host home is a room, an audience, and a recurring date.

From the person who started it

"I had been performing piano at a senior home on my own for a while before I realized the limiting factor was never interest from residents. It was that there was only one of me."

I'm Adam, a student in Calgary studying piano through the RCM program alongside school. HarmonYouth Foundation started as a way to turn occasional solo visits into something that could happen on a real, recurring schedule, with more musicians than just me.

The goal isn't a one-time recital. It's a standing date that residents and homes can plan around, and a steady outlet for student musicians who want an audience outside of competitions and exams.

If you're a student musician interested in performing, or you work at a senior home and want to set up a date, I'd like to hear from you.

AdamFounder, HarmonYouth Foundation
How a show comes together

The same four steps, every two weeks.

01

A home requests a date

An activities coordinator reaches out, or we follow up with a home we already perform at, to lock in a date.

02

The roster fills in

Musicians on our list let us know if they are available. We confirm 5 to 10 performers for that date.

03

The performance happens

A 30 to 45 minute show, usually a mix of solo and small group pieces, performed in the home's common room.

04

We follow up

With permission, we share photos from the show. It helps the next home, and the next musician, decide to say yes.
