Founder

From the person who started it

HarmonYouth Foundation began with one piano and one home.

Photo of Adam (swap for a real one)

"I had been performing piano at a senior home on my own for a while before I realized the limiting factor was never interest from residents. It was that there was only one of me."

I'm Adam, a student in Calgary studying piano through the RCM program alongside school. HarmonYouth Foundation started as a way to turn occasional solo visits into something that could happen on a real, recurring schedule, with more musicians than just me.

The goal isn't a one-time recital. It's a standing date that residents and homes can plan around, and a steady outlet for student musicians who want an audience outside of competitions and exams.

If you're a student musician interested in performing, or you work at a senior home and want to set up a date, I'd like to hear from you.
